Sakina Sheikh

On Saturday 19th May Labour members have to choose who will become the Labour candidate for Lewisham East.

I want to be that candidate before, with your help, becoming Labour’s next member of parliament for Lewisham East. A lifelong Lewisham resident and long committed to values of social justice and equality, I joined the Labour party after being inspired by the leadership of Jeremy Corbyn. In the two years since I am grateful to have been part of a movement which has begun to remake politics for the many.

Give us your details to make sure Sakina is Lewisham East's next Labour MP

More about me

I’m a local councillor, who was born and bred in the borough. Lewisham is where I have always lived and is a place which has given me everything - so now I want to give something back. I know the problems that need dealing with locally such as the shambolic service offered by Southern Rail and increasingly unaffordable housing in the area. In resisting cuts to local services I have been on the front line of fighting austerity, campaigning to ensure Lewisham Hospital services are protected, properly funded and out of private hands.

I will work hard to amplify the voices of marginalised communities in Lewisham East. While Lewisham is one of the most diverse places in Britain - a vision of our multicultural future - our parliament is not. As a young Pakistani woman who understands the local area I will make it my mission that all constituents, regardless of colour or creed, know they have an advocate at Westminster.

I’m a seasoned campaigner at the local and national level, who has skills and experience to advocate for you in parliament. From my work in climate change and energy campaigning I understand the challenges we face in tackling a system rigged for the few. Most importantly, I know tackling corporate power and inequality starts at the local level. I have nationally coordinated Keep Our NHS Public campaigns and believe our NHS must stay public. I will fight to ensure Lewisham Hospital services stay protected, properly funded and public. I will fight hard against creeping privatisation of our NHS, such as the proposed STP for South East London.
